Estonian

Contributed by Merit Niinemägi

Irina (Pavlovna) Külmoja provided some preliminary data in 2013. In 2020, Merit Niinemägi provided and analyzed the current version of the data. She used the English version of the questionnaire.

How to cite

Niinemägi, Merit. 2021. Bivalent patterns in Estonian. In: Say, Sergey (ed.). BivalTyp: Typological database of bivalent verbs and their encoding frames. St. Petersburg: Institute for Linguistic Studies, RAS. (Data first published on December 12, 2021; last revised on December 12, 2021.) (Available online at https://www.bivaltyp.info, Accessed on )

80. cut oneself vigastama

Valency pattern: NOM_PART
X: NOM
Y: PART
Locus: Y
Pearu vigasta-s ennast habemenoa-ga.
pn . sg . nom hurt- pst .3 sg refl . sg . part beard_knife- com
‘Pearu hurt himself with a razor.’
Note: I used vigastama 'to hurt' instead of lõikama 'to cut' here, because Pearu lõikas ennast 'Pearu cut himself (partitive)' would leave the impression that the cutting was done on purpose, while vigastama does not have the same connotation. Another solution would be Pearu lõikas endale ... 'Pearu cut himself (allative) ...', but in this case we'd need to specify where he cut himself to make it sound natural, e.g. kätte 'into his hand'.
