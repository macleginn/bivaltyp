## Grammar notes

### Basic clause structure and the transitive construction

Eastern Dan is a tonal language, it has six tonemes: extra-high, high, mid, low, extra-low, falling (high-extralow). The basic word order is S AUX (O) V X, where S is a subject, V is a verb, AUX is a STAMP-marker, i.e. an auxiliary word expressing grammatical semantics of tense, aspect, mode and polarity, in which the number and person of the subject are indexed (in the Mandeist tradition, AUX are named "predicative markers"), O is a direct object (whose absence makes the verb intransitive), X is an oblique (indirect object or adjunct), most often represented by a postpositional phrase. As far as the subject is indexed in AUX, the subject poistion is most often void. The word order in NP is N2 – N1 (N1 is the head noun, N2 is the dependent noun), N – Adj (the adjectival modifier follows the head noun).
 
### Case system
Within the Eastern Dan nouns, there is a subclass of declinable nouns which distinguish six cases, albeit in an irregular way.
