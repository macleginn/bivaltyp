## Grammar notes

### Basic clause structure and the transitive construction

Mano is a tonal language with three basic tones, low, mid and high. The basic word order is S AUX (O) V X, where S is a subject, V is a verb, AUX is an auxiliary expressing grammatical semantics of tense, aspect, mode and polarity, indexing the person and number of the subject; some portmanteau forms also incorporate a 3SG pronoun whose underlying position is that of direct object, argument of postposition or inalienable possessor; O is a direct object (whose absence makes the verb intransitive, except some constructions with portmanteau auxiliaries), X is an oblique (indirect object or adjunct), most often represented by a postpositional phrase. Depending on the type of temporal, aspectual and modal construction, the verb can take a specific tonal form, to which suffixes may additionally be adjoined. Certain kinds of nominalization are also marked by grammatical tone. Complex predicates consisting of a nominal and a verbal part are common; depending on their semantic and syntactic properties they can be analyzed as more or less bound forms. The word order in the NP is N2 – N1 (N1 is the head noun, N2 is the dependent noun), N – Adj (the adjectival modifier follows the head noun). With certain dependent nouns the head nouns appear in their tonal construct form.
