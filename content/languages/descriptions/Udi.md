## Grammar notes

### Basic clause structure and the transitive construction

Case marking displays the ergative-absolutive alignment. Additionally, there is Differential Object Marking (DOM). In the basic transitive construction, the A-argument is in the ergative, and the P-argument, in the absolutive when the referent is indefinite and in the dative when the referent is definite. Person names, personal and demonstrative pronouns are always marked with the dative in the P function. This is the reason why, in the abbreviations of valence patterns, the expression of the Y argument is marked as “ABS/DAT” below.
Non-core arguments are flagged by oblique case markers or, rarely, by postpositions (usually, in combination with the genitive case).
Subject personal agreement (with an S/A argument) is obligatory in independent declarative clauses. There is no gender agreement. The neutral word order is S(O)V. 
There is no copula, only personal markers are used in copular clauses. The verb bu ‘be, exist’ is used in existential, locative and possessive clauses. It tends to become fused with the preceding 3SG marker (=*ne bu* > =*no*; =*e bu* > =*o*).

### Case system
There is no clear morphological distinction between the core cases and the locative cases, which all have monomorphemic markers. The most important cases are absolutive (unmarked stem), ergative, dative and genitive. Benefactive is a peripheral non-locative case. The locative cases include ablative, adessive, superessive; they also have extended non-locative functions. 
Note that in the examples from the questionnaire, the absolutive is not marked with a gloss. 

### Verb lemmas
Verbs are cited in the infinitive form with the suffix -*es*. Stative verb *bu* ‘be, exist’, which does not possess the infinitive form, is cited in its present tense form. 

## Orthography and transcription
In the first line of examples, the Latin-based Udi writing system is used, which is adopted in Azerbaijan. The main non-trivial correspondences among the Udi letters and the linguistic transcription are: 
ə /ä/, ə̌ /aˤ/, ě /eˤ/, ǒ /oˤ/, ǔ /uˤ/, ı̌ /iˤ/
y /j/, c /ǯ/, č /ǯˤ/, j /ž/, ǰ /žˤ/, x /χ/, ğ /ʁ/
p' /pː/, t' /tː/, k' /kː/, q' /qː/

ş /šˤ/, š /šˤ/, ś /c/, s' /cː/

## Glossing abbreviations
3SG — third person singular; 3PL — third person plulral; ABL — ablative; ABS — absolutive; AD — adessive; AG — agent noun; AOR — aorist; BEN — benefactive; CAR — caritive; CVB — converb; DAT — dative; DEM — demonstrative; ERG — ergative; GEN — genitive; INF — infinitive; LV — light verb; MSD — masdar (verbal noun); NEG — negation; OBL — oblique; PERF — perfect; PF — perfective; PL — plural; PN — person name; PRS — present; SBST — substantivizer; SG — singular; ST — verb stem (part of stem, separated by an endoclitic); SUPER — superessive.

The plus sign (+) is used in cases of fusion, when the morphemeboundary cannot be established (3SG+be, ST+PRS, LV+PRS).
